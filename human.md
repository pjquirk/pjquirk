# Human User Guide
The goal of a human user guide is to make working together and collaboration with colleagues easier, faster. 
The focus for this user guide is on the workplace. 
As such, many of the questions are geared towards information that would be pertinent to my colleagues.

## About me

### What are some honest, unfiltered things about you?
* Please be direct with me, I'm pretty bad at picking up on asks that are veiled as observations.
* I'm a visual learner, please draw me a diagram when describing something complex.
* I love all personal feedback, good or bad. 
	
### What drives you nuts?
* When someone assumes I'm going to do something without an explicit ask. I can't read minds, if you need something please just tell me!
* When I'm asked to work on too many things concurrently. It's too easy to lose focus and waste time on context switching.

### What are some things that people might misunderstand about you that you should clarify?
* I tend to be quieter in group settings, it's something I work on but still have a hard time speaking up.
* I generally defer strong opinions when thinking on my feet; I prefer to research a topic before making decisions. 

### How do you treat yourself?
* Soda and chocolate are my favorites!
* I don't drink coffee or tea.

## About my colleagues

### What qualities do you particularly value in your colleagues?
* Assumes good intentions, always.
* Asks questions, dumb or not, and learns from them!
* Is patient. We all learn in different ways at different speeds, I appreciate someone who can take time to help someone understand something new.
* Be kind. We're all doing our best here :)

### What do you wish colleagues didn’t do?
* Be rigid or unwilling to compromise.
	
### How can people earn an extra gold star with you?
* Just saying "thanks" can go a long way. I get most of my job satisfaction from making other developers happy, so tell me if I do that!

## About my interactions

### How do you coach people to do their best work and develop their talents?
* When mentoring or onboarding others I usually would check in often; people generally have a million questions but don't want to bother others so bothering them first helps prompt them to ask their questions.
* Try to give them the right size work for their ability/career level and help with planning or implementation as needed.
* Bubble up good feedback to their manager or team, and bring up negative feedback privately.
	
### What’s the best way to communicate with you?
* Slack is best, but for anything complex I prefer an in-person/video conversation. Don't forget to summarize what we talked about somewhere durable/visible!
	
### What’s the best way to convince you to do something?
* Show me why another way is better and I have no problem switching to it. Strong claims require strong evidence, which goes in both directions.
	 
### How do you like to give feedback?
* I try to praise in public and offer suggestions in private, and also mention things I like to an individual's manager.
* I've been given all feedback in Slack or peer reviews, but I'm happy to talk about it whenever needed.

### How do you like to get feedback?
* Any feedback is great, however it gets to me, as long as it includes specifics. Negative feedback should include preferred alternatives or how it made someone feel.
